Merging and Splitting Stories

It’s very important to store the stories in a way that they can very easily be merged and split. It’s common for teams to record too much information in their stories. To the point that it becomes very tedious and overly complicated to attempt to merge or split a story. And so it’s rarely suggested by the team, even though it’s the right thing to do. Merging is pretty straightforward to do. And splitting stories is more of a craft, because each story still needs to aim towards following INVEST. "Flying Fingers" Estimations

Sometimes we just need a quick estimation on something, without asking everyone to use their laptops to login to a website, or passing out estimation cards to everyone. For this, we can use the simple “flying fingers” technique. We read the story, discuss it briefly, then each builder hides one hand with their estimation done in “number of fingers” to represent the size. Someone counts down 3, 2, 1, “Go”, and everyone reveals at once. ...

The INVEST Acronym : Testable

User stories are reminders of features, not full specifications. Avoid recording every detail up front because details change. We’ll capture the details later, when they matter. When a story reads like a contract, it stops being a conversation. When it reads like a reminder, it invites one. Follow the acronym “INVEST” as a quick check.
